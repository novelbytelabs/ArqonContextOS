## Purpose

Capture the product implications of MDASH-style agentic security systems for ArqonMonkeyOS and Arqon Zero.

## Core Lesson

The harness is the product.

A model can propose actions, but the durable product value comes from staged workflows, role separation, proof construction, adversarial validation, deterministic plugins, durable evidence, audit gates, and human final authority.

## Ideas to Borrow

### Proof as a First-Class Stage

Every significant claim should move through:

claim → adversarial test → differential test → proof artifact → audit decision

### Boundary Prover Layer

Add a future layer that verifies intended route works, generic bypass paths fail, role spoofing fails, malformed records fail, idempotency conflict works, source-chain mutation fails, and stale deployment is detected.

### Deterministic Governance Plugins

Encode rules as deterministic checks where possible:

- route-only artifact rules
- role authority matrix
- required diagnostic labels
- source-chain invariants
- idempotency semantics
- deployment freshness
- anti-promotion language

### Vague Task Detection

Block or request refinement when a handoff lacks target files, route names, artifact types, authority boundary, forbidden outputs, replay commands, or evidence expectations.

## What Not to Copy

Do not copy a large-agent swarm literally.

Do not allow agents to auto-patch and auto-execute without governance.

Do not treat security discovery as the only product direction.

## Product Positioning

Arqon Zero / MonkeyOS should be a truth-governed harness for agentic work.

Its moat is role separation, repo-backed evidence, route-only authority, adversarial proof, audit/debate/prove gates, deterministic plugins, and Human final authority.
